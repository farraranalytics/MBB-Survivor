02 — Taglines

Words That Hit

Three tagline options — each under 8 words, each communicating the core: one pick, survive or die. The recommended option balances drama with clarity.

High-Stakes Drama
"Every pick could be your last."

Cinematic tension. Immediately communicates the elimination format and the weight of each decision. Works as App Store subtitle, social headers, and push notifications. This is the one.

Format-Forward Clarity
"One pick per day. Last one standing."

Crystal clear. Explains the game in seven words. Best for App Store descriptions and onboarding screens where you need people to "get it" instantly.

Social Competition
"Outlast your friends. Win the Dance."

Emphasizes the social/group element. "Your friends" signals this is a group activity, not a solo game. Good for marketing but slightly softer on the stakes.

04 — Brand Voice & Personality

How We Sound

Survive the Dance sits at the intersection of high-stakes intensity and friend-group energy. Think ESPN broadcast booth meets your group chat. Confident, not corporate. Dramatic, not cheesy.

Intense but not Hostile

We talk about elimination, survival, and stakes — but it's always rooted in the fun of competition. The drama is the entertainment. We never talk down to losing players or make elimination feel cruel.

Knowing, not Nerdy

We speak fluent basketball. We reference seeds, chalk, Cinderellas, and busted brackets naturally — but we don't gatekeep. A casual fan should feel welcome, not quizzed. Smart but accessible.

Concise & Punchy

Short sentences. Active voice. Like a broadcast call, not a press release. "You survived." "Duke goes down. 4 players eliminated." Every word earns its spot — economy of language mirrors the economy of picks.

Friend-Group Energy

The app is the commissioner of your pool — authoritative but fun. It should feel like the friend who actually organized the bracket, not a faceless platform. Light trash talk is encouraged. Formality is not.

The Full Kit

Colors, type, and motifs that form a cohesive system. Designed to feel like a premium sports broadcast, not a generic fantasy app.

Weights: Regular (400), Medium (500), Bold (700). Used for: body copy, pick cards, analysis text, navigation labels, buttons. Highly legible at small sizes on mobile.

Recurring Visual Motifs

A diagonal line through eliminated teams and players. The core elimination visual — used on pick history, standings, and team lists.
◯
Court Lines
Half-court circles, free-throw arcs, and lane lines used as subtle background textures and section dividers throughout the app.
▮▮▮

A segmented progress bar showing days survived out of total tournament days. Each segment lights up as you advance — or goes dark.

A grid of 64 team slots — used fills up as you burn teams. A visual representation of your "ammo" running out over the tournament.
